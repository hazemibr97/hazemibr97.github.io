I am experienced in conducting qualitative and quantitative studies, leveraging HCI frameworks to study the human elements of technology, with an interest in the field of education. My work is primarily focused on bringing to light the hidden mechanisms by which underserved and underrepresented communities interact and utilize technology in their daily life, and developing tools to fit their needs.

Interests

I have a strong interest in pursuing Human Computer Interaction and ICTD research from an educational perspective in the Middle East and North Africa, as these regions have been considerably underrepresented in HCI literature, although they are areas with rich and unique social, cultural, and political dynamics that have recently been revolutionized through the wide-spread access of mobile devices and social media.

Apart from research, my interests include keeping active by playing basketball. I have always been involved in competitive basketball communities throughout high school and university, and have captained the varsity teams at both highschool and university levels for more than 7 years, leadinng NYUAD's varsity basketball team to consecutive national level championships in 2014 and 2015. In my free time, I enjoy reading and following the latest trends in educational technology and policy, watching sci-fi shows, and exploring the outdoors through hiking or biking.
